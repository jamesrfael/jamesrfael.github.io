Rain In Iligan

  • John Remula
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12 Comments

I was awakened by the sound of rain smashing the window of my hotel room in Iligan. It was so loud that I started to rethink my supposed trip to Marawi. When I checked my itinerary, it appears that if I don't proceed with today's trip to the Islamic city, I will have to skip it altogether and come back some other time. So what's the final decision? I packed all my stuffs, checked out at the hotel and rode a jeep to Pala-o. Jeeps bound for Marawi can be found at Iligan Southbound Terminal so from city center, transfer to a jeep going to Suarez and tell the driver to drop you at the terminal.

Rooms

  • Diego Lubas
  • Apr 12, 2021
  • 9 Comments

This mountain resort by the way is huge, which means its Maranao-inspired rooms are quite distant from one another. I attempted to walk outside and hope that there are public transportation around that would bring me to the Torogan of Dayawan. After waiting in vain under a shed beside MSU's main gate, I braved the flood and walked back to my hotel room wet, again. Good thing I have a cup of noodles ready to fill my talking tummy back at the hotel, bad thing though is I don't have hot water. I can't say I'm lucky but it was a relief when a room boy came before it got totally dark.

Mosques

  • Eric Sagarino
  • Mar 21, 2020
  • 12 Comments

I paid a visit to King Faisal Mosque (still inside MSU compound) just in front of Marawi Resort Hotel. Kuya Toks' reliever is still nowhere to be found so I went back to MSU proper and visited Aga Khan Museum which houses a good collection of everything Maranao, from instruments, weapons, house ornaments, and many other. There's no entrance fee. You will be welcomed by an amazing display of Kulintang Ensemble (local instrument made of gongs).

Tradition

  • John Doe
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12 Comments

You will also find small replicas of Maranao traditional structures such as the Masgit (Maranao Mosque), Lamin (Maranao Princess Dormitory) and Torogan (Maranao Royal House). Among three, only Torogan is left standing and you can find two of which in Barangay Tuka (Dayawan), located just beside Lake Lanao not too far from Marawi city center. I can say that this museum is one of the most precious in the country. Never miss this if you're planning a visit to Marawi.
