About Lanao Del Sur.

In 1959, Lanao was divided into two provinces, Lanao del Norte and Lanao del Sur, under Republic Act No. 2228. Marawi was designated as the capital of Lanao del Sur. The city was renamed the "Islamic City of Marawi" in 1980, and is currently the Philippines' only city having a predominantly Muslim population.

The people of Lanao used to adhere to Hinduism, polytheist animism, and Buddhism. Lanao del Sur forms the western portion of Northern Mindanao. It is bounded on the north by Lanao del Norte, on the east by Bukidnon, on the west by Illana Bay, and on the south by Maguindanao and Cotabato.

It is also redominantly agricultural where rice, corn, and coconut are the main products. Lanao Lake provides the need of the people for fish products. Cottage industries include weaving of traditional clothes and metal products as well as home furnishings.

Culture

The people of Lanao used to adhere to Hinduism, polytheist animism, and Buddhism.
